Data Availability
The data used in this study are publicly available as detailed below. Spatially
resolved impervious surface percentages were obtained from the U.S. Geologi-
cal Survey (USGS) via the 2024 Annual NLCD Collection 1 Science Products
(https://doi.org/10.5066/P94UXNTS). City-wide precipitation depths for a 2-year
1-hour storm were extracted from NOAA Atlas 14 Point Precipitation Frequency
Estimates (https://hdsc.nws.noaa.gov/hdsc/pfds/). The counts of combined sewer
overflow (CSO) outfall locations were compiled individually from various sources for
each city and are itemized by city in Supplementary Table X. Total parking lot area
percentages were generated by executing the Esri Pretrained Parking Lot Classifi-
cation (USA) Deep Learning Model (https://doc.arcgis.com/en/pretrained-models/
latest/imagery/using-parking-lot-classification-usa.htm) over high-resolution aerial
imagery from the U.S. Department of Agriculture. National Agriculture Imagery
Program (NAIP) Imagery (https://www.fsa.usda.gov/programs-and-services/
aerial-photography/imagery-programs/naip-imagery/). Geospatially resolved heat
severity above and local average is generated from 30-meter Landsat 8 imagery
band 10 captured during the summer of 2025 made accessible through ArcGIS Liv-
ing Atlas of the World (https://tiledimageservices.arcgis.com/BvefdV6XvRo2Jt72/
arcgis/rest/services/Heat Severity Urban Areas USA 2025/ImageServer). City-
wide values for Summer Nighttime Low Temperatures are obtained from
NOAA National Centers for Environmental Information Climate Normals por-
tal (reflecting the 2006–2020 August nighttime minimum temperature averages)
(https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/access/us-climate-normals/). Spatially resolved ambi-
ent air quality burdens, modeled as percentile rank of the domain consisting
of ozone, PM2.5, air toxics cancer risk, and diesel particulate matter, were
obtained from the CDC and Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Reg-
istry (ATSDR) Environmental Justice Index data catalog (https://atsdr.cdc.gov/
place-health/php/eji/eji-data-download.html). Finally, the city boundary layer
polygons used to define the spatial extent are hosted in our project repository at
https://github.com/omidemam/bgipotential/blob/main/Data/City boundaries.rar.
